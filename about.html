I am a developer and teacher.

Why Software?

I came to software from the world of education. Before enrolling in Flatiron's immersive web development program, I taught 7th Grade English for two years in Brooklyn. It wasn't until the end of my second year that I became interested in programming. I took a CS 101 course online taught by Nick Parlante. Prof. Parlante was an amazing teacher: patient, affable, and a master at predicting where misunderstandings will arise and how to explain them. He inspired me to pursue a career in development because as his student, I never wanted to stop learning.

I still love learning, and especially the adventure that is learning to program. But as with any job, you don't really know why you love it until you actually start doing it. Now that I've worked in software, I can say that the reason I want to be here is more than just my passion for code. Right now, there's a really exciting shift happening in software. Programming is evolving from an exclusive, predominantly white-male-dominated, and sometimes unfriendly sphere to an industry that is much more compassionate, welcoming, and diverse. I'm sticking around because I want to be a part of this change.
